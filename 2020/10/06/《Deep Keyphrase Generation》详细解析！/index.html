Deep Keyphrase Generation

Summary

Research Objective

find a approach which more conform to the process of keyphrase prediction on how real human annotators would assign keyhphrase.

Background and Problems

Exsiting methods for keyphrase prediction all just can extract keyprases that fully match a part of the text, leading to these methods can not capture the real semantic meaning behind the text.

Traditional keyphrase extraction algorithms can be summarized as two steps:

  1. acquire a list of keyphrase candidates
  2. rank candidates on their importance to the document

In the first step, researchers often use n-grams or noun phrases with certain part-of-speech patterns to identify potential candidates so they can only select present keyphrases as candidates. But actually, widely used datasets all have a large percentage absent keyphrases(those with a slightly different sequential order or those that use synonyms). When authors assign keyphrases, they consider the text’s semantic meaning, not following the written content.
In the second step, researchers often use machine learning features such as TF-IDF and PageRank that based on the statistics of word accurence and co-occurrence but are unable to reveal the full semantics that underlie the document content.

Automatic Keyphrase Extraction by Bridging Vocabulary Gap

Research Objective

To solve the problem that the current keyphrase extraction methods are based on statistical frequency, but the fact is that the keyphrase is not necessarily the one with the most occurrences.

Background and Problems

Exsiting methods for keyphrase extraction always extract keyphrases according to their statistical properties but actual keyphrases are not always statistically significant and even don’t appear in the given document.

Author called this vocabulary gap which means there are some differences between words in given doc and its keywords.

Method(s)

Authors use the idea of translation. They consider source doc and keyphrases are different languages so they can use word
alignment models (WAM) to learn translation probabilities between the words in the two languages. In a word, it is a unsupervised methods for keyphrase extraction and it can extract the low frequency keyphrases.

preparing translation pairs

two types of pairs

  1. title-based pairs

  2. summary-based pairs

    two types of making length balance

  3. sampling method
    score the words in document according their importance and select the first few to represent this document.

  4. split method
    split the document/summary to some sentences, then compute the similarity between sentence and title/summary’sentence. If their similarity is more than a certain number, this pairs is useful.

Evaluation

baseline

  • TF-IDF
  • TextRank
  • ExpandRank
  • LDA

    other analysis

  • title-based pairs and split method are better.
  • some analysis of the influence of parameters on the model.
  • If title and summary are unavailable, we can select the most important sentence in the document as keyphrases.
  • LDA and ExpandRank can both generate keyphrases, but WAM performs better.

    Conclusion

Note

  • WAM assums each translation pair should be of comparable length
  • 作者着重解决的问题应该是提取出现频率比较少的关键短语。大部分都在写这个，只在最后提了一下自己的模型也可以生成文中不存在的词，但我不是很懂如何生成的。看起来也像是第一个提出keyphrase generation概念的
  • 作者把翻译思想引入了关键短语抽取中，用的是1993年的模型

Keyphrase Extraction Using Deep Recurrent Neural Networks on Twitter

Research Objective

meet these challenges

Background and Problems

Previous studies about keyphrase extraction almost select keyphrase according their statistical characteristics. But for some short text such as tweets, short lengths don’t contain much statistical information.

three challenges:

  1. It’s more difficult to extract keyphrases of short texts because many linguistic and statistical features can not be used.
  2. Traditional process of keyphrase extraction has three steps. Errors are easy to propagate and even magnify in this process.
  3. keyphrase extraction of tweets lack of training and evaluation data.

    Method(s)

Evaluation

First, author crawl a big amount of tweets and use tag of tweet as keyphrase. In general, a tweet only has one tag, so it has one keyphrse. When the tag is made up of several words, author split the tag to several keyphrases.

Inputs of models are word embeddings that is pretrained on part of a Google News dataset.
