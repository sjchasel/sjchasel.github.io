    Keyphrase Prediction With Pre-trained Language Model

    Summary

    At present, keyword prediction mainly includes two tasks. Keyphrases extraction can not predict absent keyphrases and keyphrases generation can not break through the limitation of their generating ability. So author propose a framework which cotain two subtasks, PKE for extract present keyphrases and AKG for generate absent keyphrases. The results show that this model’s performance has substantial improvements.

    Research Objective

    To design a better method of keyphrases prediction considering the different characteristics of generating model and extracting model.

    Background and Problems

    Exsiting approaches of keyphrase prediction have two problems:

    1. can not generate absent words
    2. extractive model is still great potential for performance improvement
      (e.g. Adopting the copy mechanism is more effective than directly generating words from the vocabulary. However, the copying mechanism generates a word at each time step and does not take dependencies between the selected words into consideration.)

      Method(s)


      keyphrase prediction:
    3. PKE:extract present keyphrases
    4. AKG:generate absent keyphrases

    PKE:
    a sequence labeling problem using BiLSTM-CRF

    1. pre-trained BERT → contextual embedding
    2. a sentence filter → select sentences that probably have present keyphrases

    AKG:

    1. exploit the extractive information from the shared BERT model fine-tuned on the PKG
    2. a Transformer-based model with the copying mechanism:use information in present keyphrases to generate absent keyphrases
    3. a gated fusion layer in a gated fusion attention module:fuse the present keyphrase knowledge and Transformer encoder representations

      Evaluation

      Dataset

    • KP20k
    • Krapivin
    • NUS

      Baseline Models

      extractive algorithms

    • TF-IDF
    • TextRank
    • BiLSTM-CRF

      generative algorithm

    • CopyRNN
    • TG-Net
    • KG-KE-KR
    • CatSeqTG-2RF
    • KG-GAN
    • ParaNetT+CoAtt

      Evaluation Metrics

    • macro-averaged precision
    • recall
    • F-measure
    • F1@M

    Conclusion

    important module:

    PKE:

    1. sentence filter
    2. CRF

    AKG:

    1. using a fixed BERT as contextual features is better than fine-tuning it
    2. fusion attention module

      Note

      Joint Inference（联合推理方法）

      是一种集成学习的方法，构建n个模型，最后对n个模型的结果进行求和等方法来预测。

      参考：https://www.dazhuanlan.com/2020/03/19/5e737547ddc5b/

    Incorporating Copying Mechanism in Sequence-to-Sequence Learning

    Summary

    There are two ways to process information in human’s brain. One is to process information into other forms, and the other is copying which is memorizing information by rote. Existing sequence-to-sequence learning frameworks are based on the first approach. In order to add the second approach to the model and make the model more flexible, the author proposed COPYNET which add a replication mechanism in seq2seq learning. According to results, COPYNET performs well in some text processing tasks.

    Research Objective

    propose COPYNET to solve copying in seq2seq learning

    Background and Problems

    In sequence-to-sequence learning, when encountering proper nouns or names, you should output the same words but not replace them. Most of the existing methods are based on semantics, and replacing these words with other nouns may change the meaning

    Method(s)


    Encoder：a bi-directional RNN
    Decoder：an RNN decoder with following differences

    • use generate-mode and copy-mode to predict
    • use word-embedding at time t-1 and its corresponding location-specific hidden state in M to update state
    • attentive read and seletive read in reading M

    Evaluation

    task

    A synthetic dataset on with simple patterns

    不是很懂这个规则

    A real-world task on text summarization

    In this task, when summarizing a text with semantic methods, the summary generated will have many words out of vocabulary, leading to a bad performance. So author use copy mechanism to utilize words in the original text to improve results.


    COPYNET does well in generating proper names and names in abstracts and can put together segments far away from each other

    A dataset for simple single-turn dialogues.


    Even though the sentences generated by RNN have the correct format, the meaning is irrelevant answer.

    Conclusion

    The author proposed COPYNET to incorporate copying into the sequence-to-sequence learning framework. This approach performs well on some tasks such as a single discussion of dialogues and text summaries.
