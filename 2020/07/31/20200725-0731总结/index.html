KPTimes: A Large-Scale Dataset for Keyphrase Generation on News

Summary

Author find that existing datasets for keyphrase generation are always scholarly domain and have inconsistent and inaccurate annotations. These may be not benefit to models’ generalization and train. So he build a big dataset that over comes these shortcomings to help devise more robust model.

Research Objective

Build a data set which is not only has rich data for neural networks but has gold-standard labels.

Problem Statement

Keyphrase generation and keyphrase extraction has some differents such as keywords from keyphrase generation task can does not appear in the source text. But existing datasets for keyphrase generation are always scholarly domain and have not perfect annotations. The annotations from readers are always from the source text, that is the readers take the task of annotating as keyphrase extraction.The annotations from authors are always have grammatical or spelling errors.
Such annotations will arise two problems:

  • models lack capacity of generalize that makes practice or application in the other domain difficult.
  • author-assigned keyphrases exhibit strong consistency issues that negatively impacts the model’s performance.

Method(s)

KPTimes

This dataset is come from New York Times by crawling. Except for the text, HTML metadata also provide keyphrases and keyphrase variants. These are regarded as gold standard.

After crawling data, author remove duplicates and some artcles whose keypharses are too few or too many, and randomly divided it into training,development and test splits.

Using this complete dataset to train models can ensure consistency of annotation but doesn’t benefit to the model’s generalization.

HTML pages also provide some additional information such as text style properties and Off-the-shelf domain classification, that is benefit to keyphrase generation.

JPTimes

It is treated in the same way as the former, but comes from JapanTimes.

Evaluation

data analysis

The authors analyzed the data and found that

  • Professionals have larger vocabularies and write more accurate keywords
  • editors(more professional than readers) are more likely to generalize and assign keyphrases that don’t appear in the source text.

Exclusive Hierarchical Decoding for Deep Keyphrase Generation

Summary

To address the two problems of ignoring hierarchical compositionality and generating repetitive keywords, the authors propose a exclusive hierarchical decoding framework for KG. The process of hierarchical decoding is taken into account hierarchical compositionality. And there are two exclusion mechanisms (soft mechanism and hard mechanism) to avoid producing repetitive keyphrases.

Research Objective

Propose a new algorithm is proposed to solve the problems mentioned below.

Background and Problems

Keyphrase generation aims to generate a set of keyphrases that can summarize a document. But Existing approaches have some shortcomings:

  1. They can only generate N keyphrases. But actually, the number of keypharses should be determined by the content of the document.
  2. They always generate some duplicates and Removing duplicates will waste time and computing resource.
  3. The sequential decoding method ignores the hierarchical compositionality which is a keyphrase set is composed of multiple keyphrases and each keyphrase consist of multiple words existing in a keyphrases set.

    Conclusion

advantages

  • Two models proposed by author both perform very good compared with other models especially when it comes to removing duplicate keywords.
  • The model with hard exclusion mechanism called ExHiRD-h perform better, so the author makes a more detailed application of it.
  • The larger the window, the lower the DupRatio. Because When window’size is larger, it can see more keyphrases generated previously, and prevent generating the same keyphrases as before.

disadvantages:

  • Although can improve the number of unique keyphrases by a large margin, which is extremely beneficial to solve the problem of insufficient generation, the models have the problem of overgeneration.
  • Even though increasing the size of the window can reduce DupRatio of keyphrases and increase the probability of generating more accurate keyphrases, generating so many unique keyphrases also leads to more false predictions.

    强化学习(reinforcement learning)

    机器学习可分成四类：有监督学习、无监督学习、半监督学习、强化学习
    三种监督学习的区别在于是否有标签、是否所有数据都有标签
    强化学习用的样本数据和无监督学习一样，是没有标签的。但是有一个奖惩函数/回报函数，可以给我们一个延迟的反馈，让我们知道我们是否在朝着目标前进。
    比如在猜数字的游戏中，我们每猜一个数字，对方就会告诉我们大了/小了。这样可以保证我们猜的数字越来越接近正确答案。但如果是有/半监督学习，他就会说：对/错。
    参考：https://blog.csdn.net/weixin_43552312/article/details/107415857

    对隐藏层的理解

    例子：当认识一个人时，我们会综合他的各方面情况对他进行打分。这就是将特征抽象化了。如果需要根据这个总分将人具象化，就需要对分数所含的信息进行挖掘。因为一个单独的分数可能会包含着很多个指标，比如长相、身高、学历等。
    模型中的隐藏层做的就是对抽象的数据进行信息挖掘。隐藏层的层数越多，挖掘出来的特征也就越多、越独特。因此微调里也说了，用其他人训练好的模型的前几层，因为前几层一般挖掘出来的是最基础的特征。
    参考：https://blog.csdn.net/XiaoH0_0/article/details/101316159

    fine-tuning微调

    别人用大量数据训练好了一个模型。如果自己样本量不足以重新对模型进行训练，那么就可以使用微调的方法。即取其他人训练好的模型的前几层，再用自己的模型进行训练。因为前几层一般提取的是基础特征，所以可以节约时间又达到相同的效果。
    参考：https://blog.csdn.net/XiaoH0_0/article/details/101316159

GRU可以和LSTM达到差不多的效果，但是计算效率更高

嵌入

词嵌入是指把一个维数为所有词的数量的高维空间嵌入到一个维数低得多的连续向量空间中，每个单词或词组被映射为实数域上的向量。
图嵌入是一种将图数据（通常为高维稠密的矩阵）映射为低微稠密向量的过程。
